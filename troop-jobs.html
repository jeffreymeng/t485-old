Job Scout Responsibilities
Scoutmaster Trains and guides boy leaders. Works with other responsible adults to bring Scouting to boys. Uses the methods of Scouting to achieve the aims of Scouting.
Senior Patrol Leader Runs troop meetings, patrol leaders’ conference (PLC), events, and the annual program planning meeting (APM).
Assigns duties and appoints junior leaders with the advice and counsel of the Scoutmaster.
Assistant Senior Patrol Leader Helps the Senior Patrol Leader lead meetings and activities and runs the troop in his absence. Assists in the training and supervision other junior leaders.
Junior Assistant Scoutmaster Serves primarily as an Assistant Scoutmaster, except as a senior scout of 16-18 years of age. Performs duties as assigned by the Scoutmaster.
Patrol Leader Wildcats Plans and leads patrol meetings and activities, keeping patrol members informed. Assigns specific duties to patrol members, and represents the patrol at both PLCs and the APM. Knows the abilities of each patrol member, showing and developing patrol spirit along the way.

Troop Guide Wildcats Oversees the plans and events of the patrol, assisting the Patrol Leader in any means possible. Introduces new scouts to troop operations, whilst guiding them from harassment by older scouts. Helps new scouts earn ranks and teach basic scout skills.

Historian Gathers pictures and facts about troop activities and update the photo board at least once every term. Takes care of troop trophies, ribbons, and souvenirs of activities.
Quartermaster Keeps records on both patrol and troop equipment. Makes sure the troop equipment is in a clean and working condition. Makes suggestions for new or replacement items.
Scribe Attends, keeps, and sends a log of all troop meetings, PLCs, and the APM. Records individual scout attendance and advancement.
Librarian Sets up and takes care of the troop library, keeping records of pamphlets. Adds new or replacement items, and repairs the library when needed.
Instructor Teaches basic scouting skills among the entire troop. Schedules and coordinates merit badge counselors for troop instruction.
Bugler Makes appropriate bugle calls, as requested, at troop activities.
Webmaster Ensures the troop web site is stable and secure. Updates the website with information as needed or requested.
Leave No Trace Trainer Helps minimize impact by teaching troop members the principles of Leave No Trace and improving Scouts’ outdoors ethics decision-making skills.
OA Representative Attends troop and OA (Order of the Arrow) chapter meetings regularly as a youth representative, serving as a two-way communication link. Helps arrange the conduction of OA elections and inductions.
Den Chief Knows the purpose of Cub Scouting. Helps the troop’s respective Cub Scouts achieve the purposes, and serves as the activities assistant at den meetings. Sets a good example as a model scout, friendly to the boys in the den and knowledgeable of the pack meeting plans.
